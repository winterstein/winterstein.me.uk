Charlie (Candy) Colin (Curley) Touching Soft Things is an adaptation of Steinbeck's sad but beautiful book "Of Mice and Men". reset to modern England which saves us from bad american accents unfortunately it makes a mess of the ending: Tom and Pete are migrant labourers Pete is immensely strong but retarded. Tom looks after him and protects him A dream where they will get a small farm together and raise rabbits for Pete to stroke There is a moment it seems possible. The cast do a good job with most of the story however the final section Steinbeck's heartbreaking simple sparse prose doesn't translate well to the stage. Worse, the threat of lynching - which would be ridiculous in a modern english setting - is simply dropped from the plot. Without this, Tom's final act of kindness makes no sense. Nevertheless this is a competent play (although for the same money and time you could read the book).
Runs 6-11 Aug at 1:30pm (2:45). Tickets £5.50 (£4.50)
